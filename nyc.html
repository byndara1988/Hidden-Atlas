About the City

A Journey Through the Big Apple

Founded as New Amsterdam by Dutch settlers in 1624, New York City was renamed in 1664 after joining the British Empire. Over the centuries, waves of immigrants helped shape its rich, diverse culture. Iconic landmarks like the Statue of Liberty became symbols of hope and freedom for millions.

Today, NYC stands as a vibrant, bustling hub of business, art, fashion, and culture. With towering skyscrapers, a legendary food scene, and endless attractions, the city never sleeps — and it never ceases to amaze.

Whether you're visiting for the first time or returning for another adventure, this guide covers must-see places, surprising facts, and insider budget tips to help you plan your perfect New York City getaway.

Did You Know?

Surprising Facts About New York City

NYC is full of stories that most tourists never hear. Here are six of our favourites.

🏛️

First U.S. Capital

New York City served as the very first capital of the United States from 1789 to 1790, before the role passed to Philadelphia and later Washington D.C.

📰

Times Square Origins

Originally called Longacre Square, it was renamed Times Square in 1904 after The New York Times moved its headquarters into the iconic tower there.

🗽

Lady Liberty's Address

Technically, the Statue of Liberty sits in New Jersey waters — but don't worry, it firmly belongs to New York. A quirk of geography and politics!

🍕

Pizza Power

New Yorkers devour around 23 pounds of pizza per person each year. America's first pizzeria — Lombardi's, opened in 1905 — still serves slices in Little Italy.

🚇

World's Largest Subway

With 472 stations, the NYC subway is the largest in the world by number of stations. It runs 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.

✈️

Tip for Flying In

When flying from Europe, book a window seat on the left (port) side of the plane for breathtaking views of the Manhattan skyline on approach to landing.

Top Attractions

Must-See Places & Experiences

Seven unforgettable stops — from neon-lit squares to Liberty views for free.

🌃
01
📍 Midtown Manhattan

Times Square — The Neon Wonderland

An iconic symbol of NYC, Times Square dazzles with giant LED screens, street performers, and round-the-clock action. Day or night, it pulses with the energy that defines this city.

Visit after dark for the most dramatic effect — the neon glow reflecting off rain-soaked streets is a sight you'll never forget.

💡 Tip: Come on a weekday evening to avoid the biggest weekend crowds. The TKTS booth here sells same-day Broadway tickets at up to 50% off.
🏙️
02
📍 One Vanderbilt Ave

Summit One Vanderbilt — Best Views in Manhattan

Height: 1,401 ft (427 m) · 91st floor · Next to Grand Central

Zoom up to the 91st floor in just 10 seconds and enjoy immersive exhibits alongside breathtaking 360° skyline views. A must for photographers and anyone who wants to truly grasp the scale of this city.

💡 Tip: Book online in advance — tickets sell out, especially at sunset. The SUMMIT also has reflective glass floors and ASCENT, a levitating glass cabin above the rooftop.
🌆
03
🚌 All Boroughs

NYC Contrast Tour — Explore Beyond Manhattan

Go beyond the tourist trail with a guided contrast tour of Harlem, Brooklyn, the Bronx, and Queens. This unique experience gives real insight into the city's cultural and ethnic diversity — the real NYC most visitors never see.

💡 Tip: The free Staten Island Ferry runs 24/7 and gives spectacular views of the Manhattan skyline and Statue of Liberty — zero cost, completely legal, absolutely stunning.
🌉
04
📍 Brooklyn Bridge Park

Brooklyn Bridge & Chinatown Walk

Take a scenic walk across the iconic Brooklyn Bridge — one of the world's most photographed landmarks — and then head into nearby Chinatown for authentic eats and affordable souvenirs.

💡 Tip: Walk from Manhattan to Brooklyn for the best views. Sunrise and golden hour are spectacular. Chinatown is steps from the bridge for cheap dumplings and local shopping.
🎭
05
🎟️ Theater District

Broadway Shows — Magical Nights on Stage

No trip to NYC is complete without a Broadway show. The performances here are world-class — and with the right app, they don't have to break the bank.

💡 Tip: Use TodayTix to score tickets from just $20. Drinks inside theaters are expensive, but buying a souvenir cup gets you $5 off your next drink. Check for matinee deals too!
🏨
06
🎬 Movie Locations

Home Alone 2 — Movie Locations Trail

Follow in Kevin McCallister's footsteps across three iconic NYC stops. The Plaza Hotel on Fifth Avenue was used for famous scenes — explore the stunning lobby. Nearby, St. Patrick's Cathedral offers Gothic architecture and peaceful vibes, while FAO Schwarz toy store is a fun stop for all ages.

💡 Tip: The Plaza Hotel lobby is open to non-guests — walk in for a look. FAO Schwarz is steps away and worth 30 minutes of your time even as an adult.
🗽
07
⛴️ Staten Island Ferry

Statue of Liberty — Free View from the Ferry

The Staten Island Ferry is a completely free, scenic way to see Lady Liberty from the water. Running 24/7 between Whitehall Terminal in Manhattan and St. George on Staten Island, it's one of the best budget travel hacks in all of New York.

Once on Staten Island, explore nearby Snug Harbor Cultural Center and the Staten Island Museum for a local's perspective on the city.

💡 Tip: Stand on the right side of the boat (facing forward) for the clearest views of the Statue of Liberty on the trip from Manhattan to Staten Island.
Getting Around

Navigating New York City 🚇

📱

OMNY — NYC's New Fare System

No more MetroCards! NYC now uses OMNY — a contactless tap-and-go system. Simply tap your bank card, phone, or smartwatch at the turnstile and you're on.

No app or registration needed — just tap and travel.

Cost: $2.90 per ride

Weekly Cap: Once you spend $34 in a 7-day period, all remaining rides are free

Reduced Fare Cap: $17 for eligible riders

🚇

Subway Tips

The NYC subway runs 24/7 — a huge advantage over most cities. With 472 stations across all five boroughs, it gets you almost anywhere.

Download the official MYmta app or use Google Maps to plan routes in real time.

🚌 Buses also accept OMNY and are useful for crosstown trips where the subway doesn't reach.

🚕 Cabs & Uber are available everywhere but add up fast — save them for late nights or luggage-heavy trips.

✈️

Getting from the Airport

From JFK, take the AirTrain ($8.25) to Jamaica Station, then the subway into Manhattan — fast and cheap. From Newark (EWR), the NJ Transit train connects to Penn Station in about 30 minutes. Avoid taxi touts and always use official stands or rideshare apps for transparent pricing.
